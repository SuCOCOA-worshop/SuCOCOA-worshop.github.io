About the Workshop

The HiPC Workshop on Sustainable Compilers Operating Systems and Computer Architecture (SuCOCOA) aims to be the premier academic and industry forum for showcasing sustainability initiatives in the multidisciplinary computer systems research areas spanning hardware, software, and their interaction.

The workshop will highlight research work in the areas and intersection of computer architecture, operating systems and compilers that contribute significantly in upholding one or more of the UN Sustainable Development Goals. The SDGs that will be promoted in the workshop are:

🔋 Affordable and Clean Energy

Research focusing on energy-efficient computing solutions and sustainable power management.

🏭 Industry Innovation and Infrastructure

Innovative approaches to computer systems design and infrastructure development.

🏙️ Sustainable Cities and Communities

Computing solutions that contribute to building sustainable urban environments.

♻️ Responsible Consumption and Production

Efficient resource utilization and sustainable computing practices.

Areas of Interest

The workshop invites submissions in areas applicable to computer systems (from small, ultra-low power devices to large scale cluster computers, supercomputers and data centers). Areas include but are not limited to:

🖥️ Compute Platforms

Existing, emerging, novel, classical and non-traditional compute platforms at all scales

⚡ Advanced Architectures

Multicore, heterogeneous, domain-specific, IoT, mobile, embedded, reconfigurable architectures, and accelerators

☁️ Distributed Systems

Cloud and cluster computing, warehouse scale computing, datacenters

💾 Memory & Storage

Memory hierarchy, emerging memory technologies (PMEM, CXL, HBM), networking, storage, filesystems, I/O

🔧 Performance & Optimization

Performance, power, energy, security, area, cost, complexity, productivity, reliability, sustainability

🛡️ Security & Reliability

Security, reliability, availability, concurrency, virtualization and virtualized systems

Submission Guidelines

We seek original, high-quality research submissions that improve and further the knowledge of industry-academia in computer systems areas. All types of classical, non-traditional, inter/multi-disciplinary, emerging topics are welcome.

📋 Review Process

Papers will be evaluated on the following metrics to ensure high quality and relevance:

  • Novelty/Hypothesis: Original contribution and research question
  • Literature Survey: Comprehensive review of related work
  • System Design: Quality of proposed solution
  • Experimental Validation: Evidence supporting hypothesis
  • Sustainability Relevance: Connection to sustainability themes
  • Quality Assurance: Originality and plagiarism checks
